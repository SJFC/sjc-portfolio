About

Making data more human, more situated and more honest.

My work sits between data humanism, participatory consultation, critical data studies and practice research. I use poetic, material and ecological forms to question how people are represented by institutional systems, and to design more humane ways of gathering, interpreting and communicating complex information.

I work at the sharp edge of regulation, with data about lives, access, confidence, belonging and care. This is not abstract territory for me. My practice is shaped by lived experience of precarity, and by an understanding that forms, categories, thresholds and judgements can have material consequences.

The three case studies on this site —Minor Deities,Botanica and Revealing Layers — make one connected argument: how we represent lived experience can change our social worlds. Classification without context or room for difference risks developing structures of containment rather than care.

professional experience

Where policy meets practice

Scholarship, Equality, Diversity and Inclusion Lead Hereford College of Arts · 2018–present

I have worked as a tutor, lecturer in Critical Studies and manager at HCA. I currently lead institutional strategy across inclusion in higher education policy, equality, ethics and Access and Participation planning. I authored the college's Access and Participation Plan using participatory and data humanist methods alongside standard data analysis. This was approved first time by the Office for Students. I additionally led the Teaching Excellence Framework submission that contributed to the award of TEF Silver in 2019. The consultation practice documented in Revealing Layers gives an example of my working methods.

Scholarship Development Manager Association of Colleges Scholarship Project · 2015–2018

I developed and embedded scholarship within three Further Education colleges, mentoring colleagues across the sector to develop, evaluate and disseminate research.

Situated knowledge

Where the practice comes from

I come from a rural, lower socio-economic background and worked in schools situated in areas of acute rural deprivation whilst raising two children and developing my poetic practice by writing contemporary haiku (one of which was shortlisted for the Haiku Society 'Touchstone' award. My career progressed towards management, and included external consultancy work and working at a national level on the Teaching Excellence Framework subject pilot, whyilst simultaneously developing my creative and poetic practice.

In the last five years I have worked to bring both practices together, leaning on my experience of living inside the categories institutional data uses to describe disadvantage, and developing methods which enable arts and humanities practice to work with data science within legislative structures. Data humanism, for me, is not a metropolitan aesthetic: it is a way of enabling lived experience and abstract data to be listened to simultaneosly, developing structures of care through layered classifications.

Writing, images, research

Published practice

My poetry is published in Rattle, The Adroit Journal, Sugar House Review and other journals, and has been shortlisted for the Haiku Society Touchstone Award. My collage practice — found imagery, classification systems, natural history aesthetics — appears in journals including The American Psychoanalyst.

The thinking in this practice fed five accepted questions into Humanity's Last Exam, a benchmark of what current AI cannot answer, cited in Nature (2026). My doctoral research into critical and radical rurality and specialist arts education is intentionally paused, with a planned relaunch in 2027, refocused on data humanism and institutional representation.

I am a Fellow of the Royal Society of Arts, a member of the Data Visualisation Society, and a member of the Transforming Access and Student Outcomes theme working group. I hold a PGCert in Research Practices (Birmingham City University, 2021) and am currently completing an MDes in Design at the University of Sunderland — of which this portfolio is a part.
