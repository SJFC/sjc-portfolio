Participation and privacy

How your response will be used

Your response forms part of a participatory artwork. Original submissions are kept by the artist for one week and then deleted.

Who receives your response

Responses are received and read by the artist, Sarah-Jane Crowson. They are processed through Formspree, a third-party form provider that describes its service as GDPR compliant.

Why responses are collected

Responses form part of these participatory artworks and are not being collected as formal academic research. If you give permission on the form, non-identifying excerpts may inform the artist’s reflective practice or be retained and quoted in the artwork, exhibition materials or documentation of the work.

Identifying information

Please do not include identifying information in a response. The Botanica form offers the option of giving a name or initial; this is not required. Any excerpt will be used without that name or initial unless you agree otherwise.

How long responses are kept

Original submissions are kept by the artist for one week and then deleted from the artist’s Formspree account and email records. A non-identifying excerpt selected for the artwork or its documentation may be retained after the original submission has been deleted.

Asking for earlier deletion

You can ask for your original response to be deleted before the end of the week by emailing sarahjanecrowson@gmail.com. Please quote enough of the response, or its approximate submission time, for the artist to identify it.

Services used by this website

Formspree processes form submissions and may process associated technical information, including an IP address and browser information, under its privacy policy. Formspree’s services are hosted in the United States and it says it relies on Standard Contractual Clauses for transfers made as a data processor.

This website is hosted using GitHub Pages. GitHub states that it logs visitors’ IP addresses for security purposes. Further information is available in the GitHub Privacy Statement.
